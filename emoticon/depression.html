Brief Description

Depression is mental illness where has major negative feelings about themselves that interferes with daily life. Some symptoms of depression includes a persistent sad or "empty" mood, loss of interest in hobbies, feeling restless, being sluggish, fatigue, difficulty concentrating and/or making decisions, appetite/weight changes, feeling worthless or guilty, and/or suicidal thoughts. There are many different types of depression depending on how it affects them. For example, if someone is ailed by a few symptoms, they have subsyndromal depression, while if someone's ailed by most of the symptoms. Someone suffering with a form of psychosis (like delusions and/or hallucinations) while depressed has psychotic depression, while someone who is depressed during the winter months has seasonal affective disorder. Some women may get postpartum depression (where they feel depressed following pregnancy), while those with bipolar disorder will have a period of depression, but will also have a period of euphoria. Some people have symptoms of depression for well over a year, which is called "persistent depressive disorder." Some factors that often leads to depression consist of genetic, biological, environmental, and psychological factors.
Treatments

No matter how severe someone's depression is, they can still be treated effectively. Ways to do this are by using anti-depressants and/or psychotherapy (like cognitive-behavioral therapy interpersonal therapy, or problem-solving therapy). Anti-depressants may take between two and 4 weeks to work, and sometimes during this period people had an increase in suicidal thoughts. If these don't work, then they should try out electroconvulsive therapy or some other brain stimulation therapy. Before taking any option, a medical professional should be consulted.
